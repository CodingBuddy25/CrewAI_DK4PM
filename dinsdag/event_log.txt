 # In-Depth Report: The State and Future of Process Mining in 2025

## Introduction

Process mining has rapidly evolved from a niche analytical technique to a core pillar of digital transformation and operational excellence for modern enterprises. As of 2025, the integration of artificial intelligence (AI), automation, and real-time analytics has propelled process mining into mainstream adoption, with organizations across industries leveraging it to drive efficiency, transparency, and competitive advantage. This report synthesizes the latest facts, figures, and expert insights from leading industry sources, including Deloitte’s Global Process Mining Survey 2025, Gartner’s 2025 Magic Quadrant for Process Mining, and additional authoritative analyses. The aim is to provide a comprehensive, actionable perspective on how process mining is shaping business strategies, the challenges and opportunities it presents, and concrete recommendations for companies seeking to maximize its value.

---

## The Maturation of Process Mining: From Discovery to Autonomous Optimization

### Mainstream Adoption and Expanding Use Cases

Process mining has transitioned from an exploratory technology to an enterprise-wide solution, with adoption rates and satisfaction levels reaching new highs. According to the Deloitte Global Process Mining Survey 2025, nearly half (49%) of respondents report increased satisfaction with process mining in the past year, and a significant 80% agree that it delivers tangible added value ([Deloitte, 2025](https://www.processexcellencenetwork.com/process-mining/articles/6-takeaways-deloitte-global-process-mining-survey-2025)). This satisfaction is driven by both analytical value (process transparency, monitoring, and automation opportunity identification) and monetary value (cost savings, process optimization, and stakeholder satisfaction).

The scope of process mining has expanded well beyond traditional finance and supply chain processes. Financial institutions, for example, now use process mining to enhance anti-money laundering (AML) and know-your-customer (KYC) processes, while manufacturers leverage it for quality control and logistics optimization ([Marketing Scoop, 2024](https://www.marketingscoop.com/ai/process-mining/)). The technology’s ability to provide end-to-end visibility and root cause analysis is enabling organizations to address strategic objectives such as compliance, customer experience, and operational resilience.

### Integration with AI and Automation

A defining trend in 2025 is the deep integration of process mining with AI and automation technologies. Modern platforms, such as Celonis and SAP Signavio, are embedding agentic AI to move beyond passive analytics and deliver autonomous, real-time process optimization ([Business Outstanders, 2025](https://businessoutstanders.com/editors-choice/top-process-mining-tools-2025-ai-autonomous-workflows)). These systems not only detect inefficiencies but also recommend, simulate, and implement process improvements autonomously, orchestrating workflows across ERP, CRM, and bespoke systems.

This convergence is transforming process mining from a diagnostic tool into the “neural core” of autonomous enterprise operations. The result is a closed-loop system where process mining, AI, and automation continuously learn from operational data, recalibrate processes, and optimize performance with minimal human intervention ([Reworked, 2025](https://www.reworked.co/digital-workplace/why-adding-ai-to-process-mining-makes-so-much-sense/)).

## Market Growth and Vendor Landscape

### Market Expansion and Investment Trends

The process mining market is experiencing robust growth. Gartner reports that global spending on process mining software grew by over 30% in 2024, with revenues reaching $871.6 million in 2023. The market is projected to surpass $2 billion by 2028, representing an 18% compound annual growth rate ([PEX Network, 2025](https://www.processexcellencenetwork.com/process-mining/articles/highlights-gartners-2025-magic-quadrant-process-mining)). By 2026, Gartner predicts that 25% of global enterprises will use process mining platforms as a foundation for digital twins and autonomous business operations ([IBM, 2025](https://www.ibm.com/new/announcements/ibm-named-a-leader-in-the-2025-gartner-magic-quadrant-for-process-mining)).

## Business Value and Real-World Impact

### Quantifiable Benefits

Process mining delivers measurable improvements across key business metrics. Real-world examples from [Marketing Scoop (2024)](https://www.marketingscoop.com/ai/process-mining/) highlight the following outcomes:

- **Manufacturing:** 37% reduction in product defects, 45% improvement in quality productivity.
- **Banking:** 22% reduction in AML false positives, 42% improvement in investigation efficiency.
- **Energy:** 20% reduction in lead time, 14% improvement in on-time delivery.
- **Retail:** 29% reduction in order processing costs, lead time cut from 4.5 to 2.8 days.
- **Insurance:** 28% faster claims settlement, 17% increase in customer satisfaction.

These results underscore process mining’s ability to drive both operational efficiency and strategic business outcomes.

### Evolving Expectations and Strategic Alignment

Expectations of process mining are shifting. While process improvements remain a top priority (61% of respondents), cost savings (59%), process transparency (51%), and process monitoring (41%) are increasingly important. There is also a growing focus on worker productivity (36%) and customer satisfaction (28%), reflecting a broader strategic value proposition ([Deloitte, 2025](https://www.processexcellencenetwork.com/process-mining/articles/6-takeaways-deloitte-global-process-mining-survey-2025)).

## Challenges and Critical Success Factors

### Persistent Adoption Barriers

Despite its promise, process mining adoption faces several challenges:

- **Data Quality:** Inconsistent or incomplete event logs can limit insights.
- **Cross-Departmental Collaboration:** Siloed data and processes hinder end-to-end analysis.
- **Management Buy-In:** Lack of executive support remains a barrier (41% cite management attention as an issue).
- **Budget Constraints:** 24% of organizations report budget as a limiting factor ([Deloitte, 2025](https://www.processexcellencenetwork.com/process-mining/articles/6-takeaways-deloitte-global-process-mining-survey-2025)).

### Four Critical Levers for Maximizing Value

Deloitte’s 2025 survey identifies four key levers to unlock the full potential of process mining:

1. **Centers of Excellence (CoE):** Establishing dedicated CoEs drives consistent results and best practice sharing.
2. **Executive Support:** Securing leadership buy-in is essential for scaling and sustaining initiatives.
3. **AI and Automation Integration:** Embedding AI and automation delivers advanced insights and autonomous optimization.
4. **Continuous Process Optimization:** Ongoing monitoring and improvement maintain momentum and value realization ([Deloitte, 2025](https://www.deloitte.com/de/de/services/financial-advisory/research/global-process-mining-survey.html)).

## Future Outlook: Autonomous and Intelligent Process Mining

### Autonomous Operations and Agentic AI

By 2025 and beyond, process mining is expected to become the foundation of autonomous enterprise operations. The next generation of platforms will feature agentic AI capable of real-time orchestration, adaptive workflow optimization, and self-healing processes ([Business Outstanders, 2025](https://businessoutstanders.com/editors-choice/top-process-mining-tools-2025-ai-autonomous-workflows)). This evolution will see process mining embedded directly into enterprise architecture, orchestrating decisions and actions across all business systems.

### Sustainability and Operational Resilience

Process mining also plays a critical role in sustainability and resilience. By uncovering inefficiencies and compliance issues, it helps organizations minimize resource waste and environmental impact. Moreover, operational resilience is enhanced through the ability to sense, model, and adapt processes rapidly in response to changing business conditions ([PEX Network, 2025](https://www.processexcellencenetwork.com/process-mining/articles/gartner-magic-quadrant-process-mining)).

## Recommendations for Companies

Based on the latest research and market trends, the following recommendations are provided for companies seeking to leverage process mining for maximum impact:

1. **Prioritize Integration:** Select platforms with robust AI, automation, and system integration capabilities to ensure seamless orchestration across your digital infrastructure.
2. **Establish a Center of Excellence:** Build a dedicated team to drive adoption, share best practices, and scale initiatives across the organization.
3. **Focus on Data Quality:** Invest in data management and governance to ensure accurate, comprehensive event logs.
4. **Secure Executive Sponsorship:** Engage leadership early to champion process mining as a strategic priority.
5. **Adopt a Phased Approach:** Start with high-impact, well-documented processes and expand incrementally based on demonstrated value.
6. **Emphasize Continuous Improvement:** Move beyond one-off projects to a culture of ongoing monitoring, analysis, and optimization.
7. **Align with Strategic Objectives:** Ensure process mining initiatives are directly linked to business goals such as cost reduction, customer satisfaction, compliance, and sustainability.

## Conclusion

Process mining in 2025 stands at the intersection of AI, automation, and digital transformation, offering unprecedented visibility and control over business operations. The technology’s rapid maturation, expanding use cases, and integration with intelligent automation position it as a foundational capability for future-ready enterprises. Companies that invest strategically in process mining—prioritizing integration, governance, and continuous improvement—will be best positioned to unlock sustainable competitive advantage and operational excellence.
